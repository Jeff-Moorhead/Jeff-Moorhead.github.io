About Me

Software Engineer in the New York/New Jersey area

I love the challenge of developing software. That is why I do it! It's not just the act of typing code that I enjoy: it's the challenge of finding the best way to solve a problem. I think of computer science as a way to apply my passion for mathematics and science. After all, isn't code based on logic? To me it's all related, and that is one of the main aspects of computing that I find most enjoyable: the ability to apply information from any field to make software work.

In addition to my pursuits as a developer, I am passionate about music. I began playing the violin at the age of nine, and quickly took to it. At 11, I took up the mandolin, and then at 13, I took up the guitar. Currently, the guitar is my main focus, particularly folk and bluegrass guitar. Some of my most profound musical influences are Doc Watson, Tommy Emmanuel, Warren Haynes, Tyler Childers, and Jason Isbell. I play almost every day and am always looking for new people to share my music with!
